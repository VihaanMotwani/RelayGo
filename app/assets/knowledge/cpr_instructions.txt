CPR INSTRUCTIONS - VERIFIED EMERGENCY PROCEDURES

ADULT CPR (Age 12+):
1. CHECK the scene for safety
2. CHECK responsiveness - tap shoulders and shout "Are you okay?"
3. CALL for help - have someone call 911 if possible
4. CHECK breathing - look for chest rise for no more than 10 seconds

IF NOT BREATHING OR ONLY GASPING:
5. Place person on firm, flat surface on their back
6. Place heel of one hand on center of chest (between nipples)
7. Place other hand on top, interlace fingers
8. Position shoulders directly over hands, arms straight
9. Push HARD and FAST:
   - Compress at least 2 inches deep
   - Rate: 100-120 compressions per minute
   - Allow full chest recoil between compressions
10. After 30 compressions, give 2 rescue breaths:
    - Tilt head back, lift chin
    - Pinch nose shut
    - Create seal over mouth
    - Blow for 1 second, watch for chest rise
11. Continue 30:2 cycle until:
    - Person starts breathing
    - AED becomes available
    - Professional help arrives
    - You are too exhausted to continue

HANDS-ONLY CPR (if untrained or uncomfortable with rescue breaths):
1. Push hard and fast in center of chest
2. Do NOT stop - continuous compressions at 100-120/min
3. This is STILL effective and better than doing nothing

CHILD CPR (Age 1-12):
- Use one or two hands for compressions
- Compress about 2 inches deep
- Same 30:2 ratio

INFANT CPR (Under 1):
- Use 2 fingers on breastbone just below nipple line
- Compress about 1.5 inches deep
- Cover both mouth and nose for breaths
- Same 30:2 ratio

AED (Automated External Defibrillator):
1. Turn on AED and follow voice prompts
2. Attach pads to bare, dry chest as shown on pads
3. Ensure no one is touching the person
4. Press SHOCK button if advised
5. Immediately resume CPR after shock
