GENERAL EMERGENCY PREPAREDNESS - VERIFIED PROCEDURES

EMERGENCY KIT ESSENTIALS:
- Water: 1 gallon per person per day for at least 3 days
- Food: 3-day supply of non-perishable food
- Flashlight and extra batteries
- First aid kit
- Whistle to signal for help
- Dust masks and plastic sheeting
- Moist towelettes, garbage bags, plastic ties
- Wrench or pliers to turn off utilities
- Manual can opener
- Local maps
- Cell phone with chargers and backup battery

EMERGENCY COMMUNICATION:
1. Designate an out-of-area contact person everyone calls
2. Text messages often work when calls do not
3. Social media can reach many people quickly
4. Keep a written list of important phone numbers
5. Battery-powered or hand-crank radio for official updates
6. If cell towers are down, move to higher ground for better signal

WHEN TO CALL 911:
- Life-threatening emergencies
- Fire
- Serious injury or illness
- Crime in progress
- When in doubt, CALL - dispatchers will help prioritize

WHEN CELL NETWORKS ARE DOWN:
1. Try text messages first - they use less bandwidth
2. Try Wi-Fi calling if available
3. Go to a designated emergency communication point
4. Use mesh networking apps if available
5. Use walkie-talkies or ham radio if available
6. Leave visible signals (SOS, bright cloth) for aerial rescue

PSYCHOLOGICAL FIRST AID:
1. Ensure physical safety first
2. Speak calmly and clearly
3. Listen without judgment
4. Help people connect with loved ones
5. Provide practical assistance (water, blanket, phone)
6. It is normal to feel scared, confused, or overwhelmed
7. Reassure people that help is coming
8. Do NOT say "I know how you feel" or minimize their experience
9. For children: stay calm, give simple honest information, maintain routines

TRIAGE BASICS (Multiple Casualties):
Priority 1 (Immediate/Red): Life-threatening but survivable with treatment
Priority 2 (Delayed/Yellow): Serious but can wait for treatment
Priority 3 (Minor/Green): Walking wounded, can wait
Priority 4 (Expectant/Black): Unlikely to survive
- Treat highest priority first
- Reassess continuously as conditions change
