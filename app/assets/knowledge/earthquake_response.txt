EARTHQUAKE RESPONSE - VERIFIED EMERGENCY PROCEDURES

DURING AN EARTHQUAKE:
IF INDOORS:
1. DROP to your hands and knees
2. Take COVER under a sturdy desk, table, or against interior wall
3. HOLD ON until shaking stops
4. Do NOT run outside during shaking - falling debris is the greatest danger
5. Do NOT stand in doorways (this is a myth - doorways are no stronger)
6. Stay away from windows, mirrors, heavy furniture, and fireplaces
7. If in bed, stay there - protect head with pillow

IF OUTDOORS:
1. Move to an open area away from buildings, power lines, and trees
2. Drop to the ground and cover head and neck with arms
3. Stay in open area until shaking stops

IF IN A VEHICLE:
1. Pull over to side of road and stop
2. Avoid bridges, overpasses, power lines, buildings
3. Stay inside vehicle - it provides protection from falling debris
4. Set parking brake
5. After shaking, proceed cautiously - watch for road damage

IF NEAR COAST:
1. After shaking stops, move to high ground IMMEDIATELY
2. A tsunami may follow an earthquake - do NOT wait for official warning
3. Go at least 100 feet above sea level or 2 miles inland
4. Do NOT return to coast until authorities declare it safe

AFTER AN EARTHQUAKE:
1. Expect aftershocks - be ready to DROP, COVER, HOLD ON again
2. Check yourself and others for injuries
3. Check for gas leaks (smell, hissing) - if found, open windows, leave, call 911
4. Check for fire and electrical damage
5. Do NOT use elevators
6. If trapped under debris:
   - Do NOT light a match (gas leak risk)
   - Cover mouth with cloth
   - Tap on pipe or wall so rescuers can hear you
   - Do NOT shout (conserve energy, avoid inhaling dust)
7. Stay out of damaged buildings
8. Use text messages instead of phone calls to keep lines clear
9. Conserve phone battery - reduce screen brightness, close apps
