FLOOD RESPONSE - VERIFIED EMERGENCY PROCEDURES

BEFORE FLOODING:
1. Know your area's flood risk and evacuation routes
2. Have emergency supplies: water, food, medications, flashlight, radio
3. Move important documents to waterproof container
4. Know how to shut off utilities (gas, electricity, water)

DURING A FLOOD:
1. Move to higher ground IMMEDIATELY if flooding is imminent
2. Do NOT walk through moving water - 6 inches can knock you down
3. Do NOT drive through flooded roads - 12 inches can float a car
4. "Turn Around, Don't Drown" - NEVER cross flooded areas
5. Stay off bridges over fast-moving water
6. If trapped in a building:
   - Go to highest floor (NOT the attic unless you have roof access)
   - Signal for help from roof or upper window
7. If trapped in a vehicle:
   - If water is rising and vehicle is sinking, unbuckle seatbelt
   - Open window (or break it) to escape
   - Climb onto roof of vehicle if you cannot reach dry ground

FLASH FLOODS:
- Can happen within MINUTES of heavy rain
- Most dangerous type of flooding
- Move to high ground immediately when warned
- Do NOT camp or park along streams or rivers in mountainous areas

AFTER A FLOOD:
1. Return home only when authorities say it is safe
2. Do NOT touch electrical equipment if wet or standing in water
3. Throw away ALL food that contacted flood water (including canned goods with damaged seals)
4. Boil water or use bottled water until water supply is declared safe
5. Watch for snakes and other animals displaced by flooding
6. Photograph damage before cleaning for insurance claims
7. Wear protective gear when cleaning: rubber boots, gloves, mask
8. Remove wet materials within 24-48 hours to prevent mold
9. Disinfect everything that got wet with bleach solution (1 cup per 5 gallons water)
