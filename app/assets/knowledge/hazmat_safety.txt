HAZARDOUS MATERIALS SAFETY - VERIFIED EMERGENCY PROCEDURES

GENERAL HAZMAT RESPONSE:
1. DISTANCE - Get as far away as possible from the source
2. UPWIND - Move upwind from the hazardous material
3. UPSTREAM - Move upstream if near water contamination
4. UPHILL - Many chemicals are heavier than air and settle in low areas

IF YOU SEE A HAZMAT INCIDENT:
1. Do NOT approach the area
2. Do NOT touch any spilled material
3. Do NOT inhale fumes, smoke, or vapors
4. Call 911 immediately and report:
   - Your location
   - What you see (spill, leak, fire, explosion)
   - Any visible placards or labels on containers
   - Number of people potentially affected

GAS LEAK (Natural Gas, Propane):
Signs: Rotten egg smell, hissing sound, dead vegetation, bubbling in water
1. Do NOT turn on/off any electrical switches
2. Do NOT use phones near the leak
3. Do NOT light matches or create any spark
4. Open windows and doors if safe to do so
5. Leave the area immediately
6. Call 911 from a safe distance
7. Do NOT re-enter until cleared by authorities

CHEMICAL EXPOSURE:
If chemical contacts SKIN:
1. Remove contaminated clothing immediately
2. Flush affected area with large amounts of water for 20+ minutes
3. Do NOT use hot water - lukewarm only
4. Do NOT apply creams or ointments

If chemical contacts EYES:
1. Flush eyes with clean water for at least 20 minutes
2. Hold eyelids open during flushing
3. Remove contact lenses if present
4. Seek medical attention immediately

If chemical is INHALED:
1. Move to fresh air immediately
2. If person is not breathing, begin CPR (see CPR instructions)
3. Do NOT enter area to rescue others without proper protection
4. Seek medical attention

SHELTER-IN-PLACE (Outdoor Chemical Release):
1. Go inside immediately and close all doors and windows
2. Turn off all ventilation: fans, AC, heating
3. Seal gaps around doors and windows with wet towels and tape
4. Go to interior room with fewest windows
5. Listen to emergency radio for updates
6. Do NOT leave until told it is safe by authorities
