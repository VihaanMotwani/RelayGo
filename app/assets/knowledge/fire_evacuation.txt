FIRE EVACUATION - VERIFIED EMERGENCY PROCEDURES

BUILDING FIRE EVACUATION:
1. STAY CALM - panic causes more injuries than fire
2. Alert others by shouting "FIRE" and activating fire alarm if available
3. Feel door with back of hand before opening:
   - If HOT: Do NOT open. Use alternate exit.
   - If cool: Open slowly, stay low
4. STAY LOW - smoke rises, cleaner air is near the floor
5. Crawl if smoke is thick - visibility may be near zero
6. Use stairs NEVER elevators
7. Close doors behind you to slow fire spread
8. Go to designated meeting point and do NOT re-enter

IF TRAPPED IN A ROOM:
1. Close the door between you and the fire
2. Seal gaps under door with wet cloth, tape, or clothing
3. Call 911 and report your exact location (floor, room number)
4. Go to window and signal for help (wave bright cloth, use flashlight)
5. Do NOT jump unless absolutely last resort
6. If you must escape through window below 3rd floor:
   - Lower yourself to arm's length before dropping
   - Aim for soft surfaces (grass, bushes)

WILDFIRE EVACUATION:
1. Leave IMMEDIATELY when ordered to evacuate
2. Wear long sleeves, long pants, boots, and a cotton mask
3. Close all windows and doors in your home (do not lock)
4. Turn off gas and propane
5. Move flammable furniture away from windows
6. Leave lights on so firefighters can see house in smoke
7. Drive slowly with headlights on and windows closed
8. Watch for fallen trees, power lines, and other debris on road

AFTER A FIRE:
1. Do NOT enter a burned building until authorities say it is safe
2. Watch for structural collapse - floors and walls may be weakened
3. Document damage with photos for insurance
4. Contact local disaster relief (Red Cross) for assistance
