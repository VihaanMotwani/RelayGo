FIRST AID BASICS - VERIFIED EMERGENCY PROCEDURES

BLEEDING CONTROL:
1. Apply direct pressure with a clean cloth or bandage
2. Do NOT remove the cloth if blood soaks through - add more layers on top
3. Elevate the injured area above heart level if possible
4. For severe bleeding, apply a tourniquet 2-3 inches above the wound
5. Note the time the tourniquet was applied
6. Do NOT remove the tourniquet once applied

SHOCK RECOGNITION AND TREATMENT:
Signs: Pale/clammy skin, rapid weak pulse, confusion, rapid breathing
1. Lay the person flat on their back
2. Elevate legs 12 inches unless head/neck/back injury suspected
3. Keep the person warm with blankets or coats
4. Do NOT give food or drink
5. Monitor breathing continuously

BURNS:
Minor (1st degree - red skin, no blisters):
1. Cool under running water for 10-20 minutes
2. Do NOT use ice, butter, or toothpaste
3. Cover with sterile non-stick bandage

Severe (2nd/3rd degree - blisters, charring):
1. Do NOT remove clothing stuck to the burn
2. Cover loosely with clean cloth
3. Do NOT pop blisters
4. Seek immediate medical attention

FRACTURES:
1. Do NOT move the injured limb
2. Immobilize the joint above and below the fracture
3. Apply ice wrapped in cloth to reduce swelling
4. Use a splint (board, rolled newspaper, sturdy stick)
5. Check circulation below the injury (pulse, color, sensation)

CHOKING (CONSCIOUS ADULT):
1. Ask "Are you choking?" - if they cannot speak, act immediately
2. Stand behind the person, wrap arms around waist
3. Make a fist with one hand, place thumb side against abdomen above navel
4. Grasp fist with other hand, thrust inward and upward
5. Repeat until object is expelled or person becomes unconscious
